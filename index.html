NYS Incentives

There are a variety of financing options for geothermal heat pumps in New York state for both residential and commercial use. NYSERDA (New York State Energy Research and Development Authority) has been given $26.5 million in state funding to install and implement these systems in New York state. Through their Ground Source Heat Pump Rebate Initiative, they will provide funding only to eligible GSHP designers and installers approved by NYSERDA. This funding is not available directly to building owners or site owners but to the specifically approved installers. Participation in NYSERDAâ€™s Ground Source Heat Pump Rebate initiative, involves GSHP professionals providing customers with a variety of benefits at lower costs, reaching communities for which this clean technology could otherwise be unaffordable.GSHP technology can provide residents and business owners with lower and less variant annual energy bills, especially for customers with fixed or low- to moderate-income and service-oriented institutions like non-profits, schools, community centers, and houses of worship. GSHP technologies also allow for greater resiliency and reliability during disruptions to grid-connected heating and cooling sources such as blackouts. Another benefit of GSHP technologies is increased comfort and health due to improved indoor air quality delivered by emissions-free technology. Additionally, GSHP technologies are a long-term solution to heating and cooling needs. Available rebates are available on a first-come, first served basis. Small systems such as those installed in single-family residences using 10 or fewer tons of cooling capacity are eligible for rebates of $1,500 per ton of cooling capacity. Large systems that are installed at non-single family residences that are more than 10 tons of cooling capacity (usually commercial buildings) are eligible for rebates of $1,200 per ton of cooling capacity. These larger systems do receive smaller rebates per ton of cooling capacity however they usually have a greater capacity, so they a proportionally larger amount of funding. There are initiative caps with the rebate regardless of the actual installed capacity. Single-family homes can receive up to $15,000 in rebates, meaning that anything over 10 tons of cooling capacity doesn't have a discounted cost, however, most systems for small homes do not exceed 10 tons. A single building can receive up to $500,000, and a site with multiple buildings can receive up to $1 million. No single designer/installer can receive more than $13.25 million in funding. Many of these rebates and initiative caps do have exceptions, which is why NYSERDA requires a home and finance assessment prior to the installation of a ground source heat pump. To make sure that the $26.5 million in funding is allocated properly the assessment is required before administering a rebate for installation. These rebates available for ground source heat pumps are part of a larger initiative in New York state. Governor Andrew Cuomo wants to decrease greenhouse gas emissions by 40% by 2040. Through the implementation of renewable energy and increasing funding towards these programs, this goal is becoming more feasible.
